Understanding COVID-19 in General

This section will cover most of the information known regarding the COVID-19 and pandemic.

What is Coronavirus?

Coronavirus is a family of viruses that are known to inflicts diseases such as common cold to more serious respiratory illness such as the SARs and MERs that have once terrorize humanity back in 2003 and 2013 respectively.

Coronavirus nowadays are usually referred to the newly emerged novel coronavirus (nCov) that are causing the pandemic since the early 2019's. The disease caused by the novel coronavirus is known globally as COVID-19, which is a portmanteau for Coronavirus Disease 2019.

As of the time of editing this article, which is 21st June of 2021, the death toll has reached a whopping 3.8 million worldwide. It is indeed disheartening to witness the mass massacre happening right here and then for almost 2 years.

What we can do to prevent and stop the chain?

There are a couple of things we actually can do to avoid being infected and stop the chain altogether.

Cover Face with Face mask

Always wear a face mask when heading out to the public. Coronavirus are spread mainly through respiratory droplets produced by infected individuals through sneezing, coughing or even breathing. Hence, face masks helps protecting yourself and others as it acts as a layer of interception that can filter and block harmful particles from being inhaled. However, do note that there is still a risk of being infected while wearing a mask, but the risk have been significantly reduced compared to not wearing at all. Wearing a face mask can help protect peoples around you in case you are infected but is asymptomatic by preventing respiratory droplets produced from propagating through the air and infecting others.

Social Distancing

Wearing mask alone is not enough to combat the virus. It must be paired with other safety precaution as well to maximize the effectiveness of it. Social distancing must be practised all the time especially in the indoor settings. Avoid places that are too small or too crowded that social distancing is practically impossible.

Wash Hands Frequently and Effectively

Wash hands regularly whenever you can especially out at the public as our hands might unknowingly touched the surface that is contaminated by the virus such as escalator handle, lift buttons, door knobs, seats and tables. Handwashing with soap in a proper way will removes any hazardous substance in general that may be detrimental to our health.

Staying at Home

Do not go out unless necessary. Staying at home during this pandemic not only helps protecting yourself and family members, it also helps the medical front-liners to ease their work load of treating COVID patients by slowing or cut off the transmission chain.

If travelling is necessary and inevitable, one's have to take precautionary measures seriously to keep yourself as safe as possible. Do adhere to the rules promulgated by the authority and be responsible and considerate to others while travelling. Return home once the errand is fulfilled.

Get Vaccinated

Perhaps one of the most compelling way to nip COVID-19 of the bud is through the global vaccination movement. By achieving herd immunity, the spread of the disease by transmission from human to human becomes harder and unlikely.

Take Taiwan for example, Taiwan is exemplary when it comes to handling the whole pandemic situation since the outbreak. However, Taiwan's case began to surge at around May 10th this year, to a all-time-high of 723 new cases on 22th May. This is because the vaccination movement in Taiwan is lackluster before the outbreak. This clearly shows that even though an area that is "COVID-free", an outbreak may surprise you any time soon if the population is not vaccinated.

Though vaccination may not guarentee complete immunity to COVID-19, it drastically reduces the development of severe symptoms that might cause lifetime damage or even death. You will most likely feel like only catching a cold if you had contracted with the disease.

Symptoms

The common symptoms of COVID-19 includes cough, fever, shortness of breath and in extreme cases, pneumonia, chest pain, loss of speech or movement and breathing difficulties may occur. If you are experiencing any of the severe symptoms above, seek for medical treatment IMMEDIATELY at hospitals of your vicinity.

Less common symptoms but also require close attention includes

  • Body aches and pains
  • Sore throat
  • Diarrhoea
  • Conjunctivitis
  • Headache
  • Loss of taste or smell
  • Skin rash
  • Discoloration of finger and toes

If you are sneezing and you suspect that you have COVID-19, please calm down. According to an article at Covid Symptom Study, sneezing is not normally a sign of COVID-19. It might just be seasonal allergies that is affecting you. HOWEVER, sneezing COULD indicate that you have COVID-19 if you have been vaccinated according to study done by ZOE COVID research. No matter what, It's the best to cover our face and avoid sneezing openly to the air.
